HyWIT

Hyderabad Workshop on Information Theory

HyWIT was started in 2017 to commemorate the 50th anniversary of the publication of the Viterbi Algorithm. Since then, academicians and industry experts have delivered tutorials/lectures on multiple aspects of coding theory, signal processing and communication systems. In this edition we will have industry experts cover two exciting fields - the use of machine learning techniques in channel decoding, and implementation aspects of quantum encryption.

The workshop will be conducted on WEBEX (link will be updated shortly)

Speaker's Detail

18:00 Hrs, Wednesday 14th, December

Dr Satya Kumar Vankayala

ML for wireless systems

Log likelihood ratio (LLR) computations are an integral part of the communication decoder design. Soft decoding, the most accurate method of computing LLR, is computationally very expensive. Industries are thus using approximations that are designed keeping in mind the hardware complexity involved in the optimization process. In this talk, the speaker will propose a neural network based computation scheme that is trained to mimic the performance of soft decoding with high accuracy. Besides training, computational complexity is reduced by disabling the weak edges in the neural network and by approximating the activation function. The degree to which the weak connections are disabled varies according to the quality of service (QoS) and QoS class identifier (QCI) tables. The proposed scheme thus offers a three-fold benefit to a firm in terms of commercialization: the design of a near-optimal low-complex LLR computation scheme that performs well in a 5G setting that demand high speed and accuracy, implementability using state-of-the-art technologies, and the flexibility of design on the basis of the QoS requirements of the customer application. In the second part of the tech talk, the speaker will give brief overview on how to choose optimal BS transmit power based on the time.

Satya Kumar Vankayala received the Masters degree in Signal Processing from Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, India, in 2008. He received his Ph.D. degree in 2018 from Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, India. Earlier he worked in Nokia, Bangalore. Currently, he is working as a Staff Engineer in Samsung R&D Institute, Bangalore. He received the best paper award in 2021 WPMC, Japan. He also received another best paper award in IEEE ANTS, 2022. He has filed more than 30 patents and published more than 25 papers in various reputed conferences.

18:30 Hrs, Thursday, 15th December

Mr Anand M

Gentle introduction to Quantum Key Distribution

In the era of advancing ICT, with a large volume of data flowing on the internet, the security of data is paramount. Quantum key distribution (QKD) enables two parties to produce and share a key that is used to encrypt and decrypt messages. Specifically, QKD is the method of distributing the key between parties. This talk is focussed on a non-mathematical intuitive introduction to this topic for both students and working professionals.

Mr. Anand M is currently Senior Research Engineer at Centre for Development of Telematics (C-DOT), Bangalore. In C-DOT has worked in RAN Systems, and OneM2M platforms. He represents C-DOT in the 5G working Group of TSDSI, India (TSDSI). He received his B.Tech degree from NIT Calicut, in the year 2009. His area of interest includes Wireless Communication and Wireless Optical Communication. He has received numerous awards from C-DOT as author and inventor for his papers and patents. He is a Senior Member of IEEE and fellow of IETE. More about him at https://in.linkedin.com/in/anandmohandas/
